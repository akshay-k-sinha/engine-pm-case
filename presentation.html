Opportunity 1: Android Parity

A 4-week sprint restores the booking completion floor on Android

The sprint

Wk 1 Auth and session persistence fix — resolve documented login-loop failures. Week 1 includes a technical spike to scope infrastructure vs. session layer root cause before committing.
Wk 2 Booking completion QA parity — full regression across Android API levels to match iOS behavior at checkout. Beta: 15% of Android users.
Wk 3 Self-serve cancellation restored — missing cancel button on Android, documented publicly. Push notification parity. Beta expands to 50%.
Wk 4 Full rollout. Monitor completion rate and crash rate daily. Auth error rate should drop to near-zero within 48 hours of release.

Success criteria

1Primary: Android booking completion rate delta vs. iOS narrows to under 5%
2Secondary: Overall mobile conversion rate improves by at least 2 percentage points
▪Leading signal: Auth error rate drops to near-zero within 48 hrs of release

* Statistical power calculation: see Appendix, Slide 12.

* 4-week timeline reflects AI-assisted development tooling (Copilot, Cursor, 2026). Week 5 is a monitoring period, not sprint scope.

Opportunity 2: FlexPro Contextual Upsell

Present at the moment of maximum intent: leading indicators let us call the test in 3-4 weeks

What we build (weeks 1-2)

1Contextual trigger logic — surface FlexPro at checkout, tied to the specific property's cancellation policy
2Dynamic copy — show FlexPro cost in terms of this booking, not the monthly subscription
3A/B infrastructure — 50/50 split: contextual prompt vs. current generic banner

Leading indicators for an early call (weeks 3-4)

Wk 1 Prompt CTR: does contextual placement drive more engagement than the generic banner?
Wk 2 Acceptance rate: of users who engage with the prompt, what share add FlexPro?
Wk 3-4 Attach rate delta: treatment vs. control. If +2pts in treatment, the mechanism is valid.

If W1 CTR and W2 acceptance are both directionally positive, we can call mechanism validity at week 3-4, not week 8.

Data cuts to analyze: by booking value (does it work better for ADR >$200/night?), user type (first-time vs. returning), cancellation policy strictness (does a strict property drive higher acceptance?).

Conversion-focused, not acquisition-dependent. Recovers GBV from sessions already in the funnel. A business travel slowdown reduces top-of-funnel volume but conversion improvements compound on whatever traffic exists.

Appendix

Statistical Power and Test Duration

Opportunity 1: Android Parity

Intentionally not a traditional A/B test. A holdback group would mean continuing to expose half of Android users to a broken booking experience. iOS behavior serves as the natural control: we measure Android completion rate before vs. after release, with iOS as the stable comparison baseline.

VariableValueNotes
Measurement approachObservational (pre/post)iOS completion rate = baseline control
Primary metricAndroid completion rateTarget: within 5% of iOS rate
Min detectable effect2 ppt improvementAlpha 0.05, power 0.80
Required sample~2,400 Android sessionsStandard normal approximation
Estimated read time5-7 days post-releaseIf 500+ daily Android sessions
Leading signal (Day 1-3)Auth error rate → ~0Crash monitoring, not statistical test
Opportunity 2: FlexPro Contextual Upsell

True A/B test. 50/50 split at checkout. Primary metric: FlexPro attach rate. Guardrail: cancellation rate. Early call via leading indicators reduces test duration.

VariableValueNotes
Baseline attach rate40%Engine confirmed (BusinessWire May 2025)
Min detectable effect+2 pptConservative threshold for mechanism validation
Required sample (per arm)~3,800 checkout sessionsAlpha 0.05, power 0.80 (two-proportion z-test)
Total sessions required~7,600 checkout sessionsBoth arms combined
Estimated full test duration5-6 weeksAt 200+ daily checkout sessions
Early call possibleWeek 3-4If CTR (W1) + acceptance (W2) both directional

Formula: n = (z_α/2 + z_β)² × [p1(1-p1) + p2(1-p2)] / (p2-p1)² where p1=0.40, p2=0.42

Note on early stopping: Using leading indicators to call a test early introduces the multiple comparisons problem. Apply sequential testing or Bayesian monitoring rather than fixed-horizon significance testing. Standard on modern A/B platforms (Optimizely, Statsig).
