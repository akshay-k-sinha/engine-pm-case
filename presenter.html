Hard Q&A
Is the rating gap really significant? 3.6 vs 4.7 could be review selection bias.
The rating itself is a signal, not the evidence. The evidence is the specificity of the complaints — auth loops after a specific login update, a missing cancellation button, booking completion failures mid-flow. These are transactional failures. You cannot dismiss them as selection bias when the same users rate iOS 4.7.
How do you know Android drives meaningful GBV if it's only 28% of mobile?
28% of 50% of $500M is $70M in annual Android-attributed mobile GBV. A 12% recoverable completion gap on that pool is $8.4M. Even at half that estimate, it is over $4M from a 4-week sprint. The field-worker sub-segment on Android also has high LTV if retained.

Hard Q&A
Why 4 weeks? Couldn't this take longer if the auth issue is infrastructure-level?
That is exactly why Week 1 is a technical spike before the full sprint commits. The spike determines whether the auth failure is in the session layer (fast fix) or shared infrastructure (longer, potentially requires architectural work). The sprint adapts based on spike findings. I would not commit 2 engineers for 4 weeks before that spike completes.
What if you only have half the engineering capacity?
Cut push notification parity from sprint scope. Items 1-3 (auth, completion, cancel) are the GBV-bearing fixes. Sprint with 1 engineer on auth and completion, defer push to Q2.

Hard Q&A
How do you know the current upsell is generic?
Based on the public-facing product audit and public screenshots. I cannot see the authenticated checkout flow without an account. This is a hypothesis grounded in what is publicly visible. Verifying it with an authenticated session before sprint kickoff takes 30 minutes. I flagged it on Slide 2.
Won't pushing FlexPro harder just increase cancellation rates?
That is exactly the guardrail metric. We track FlexPro attach rate and cancellation rate simultaneously from day one. If attach rises but cancel rate rises proportionally, we are attracting bookings that would have cancelled anyway. A well-framed contextual prompt converts users who genuinely value flexibility, and those users cancel less than users who signed up without understanding the product.

GBV Math to Know Cold

  • Base case (+8 pts): $500M × 8% × 0.75 = $30M

Hard Q&A
Is $30M realistic from a UI change?
The math is conservative, not aggressive. Contextual upsells in fintech and travel typically move 8-12 points first iteration — this uses the low end. The 40%/70% ratio is confirmed Engine data. At $300M GBV, the base case drops to $18M. Even then, a 2-week build generating $18M in GBV is a high-return investment. The mechanism bet — contextual beats generic at maximum intent — holds at any GBV baseline.
Why not run the full 8 weeks for proper statistical power?
At 200+ daily checkout sessions, full power arrives in 5-6 weeks anyway. Leading indicators at week 3-4 accelerate the business decision point without replacing the statistical test. We use Bayesian monitoring or sequential testing (mSPRT) to enable valid early stopping, not raw p-value peeking. See Appendix for detail.

11 / 12 Metrics + Close — Three levers, one outcome
Talk Track

"Three metrics drive GBV. Mobile booking completion rate — more sessions convert to bookings. FlexPro attach rate at checkout — each booking is worth more. And 90-day repeat booking rate — users return without acquisition cost. These three are the story. GBV is the outcome."

"The guardrails are simple: support tickets per booking must not rise as we speed up checkout, and cancellation rate must not worsen as we push FlexPro harder. That is the Q1 plan. Fix the conversion floor. Make FlexPro contextual. Thank you."

Metric Definitions
  • CVR (Mobile booking completion rate): Completed bookings / total mobile booking sessions. Measured by platform: iOS and Android separately.
  • ATR (FlexPro attach rate): Bookings with FlexPro / total completed bookings at checkout, not account level.
  • RET (90-day repeat booking rate): Users with 2+ bookings in 90 days / users with at least 1 booking.
  • GBV: Gross Booking Value — total hotel spend processed through Engine. North star.
  • ADR: Average Daily Rate — average room revenue per occupied room per night.
Hard Q&A
What is $30-35M in net revenue, not GBV?
At Engine's commission model — approximately 10-15% of booking value from hotel partners — $30-35M in GBV converts to $3-5M in net revenue. Meaningful from two Q1 bets. And it compounds: FlexPro users at 1.75x spend generate higher-margin bookings in future quarters.

APPENDIX A/B Test Power Calculations
When to Use This Slide

Reference only if directly asked about test duration, sample size, or statistical methodology. Do not navigate here proactively — it is reference material, not part of the main narrative.

Hard Q&A — Why No A/B Test for Android?
Why don't you run a proper A/B test for the Android fix?
A holdback group would mean deliberately continuing to expose half of Android users to a broken booking experience — auth loops, missing cancel button, completion failures. That is not ethically defensible when the fix is ready. Instead: iOS behavior is the natural control. We measure Android completion rate before vs. after release, with iOS as the stable baseline. If Android improves materially while iOS holds flat, the fix is validated. Leading signal: auth error rate drops to near-zero within 48 hours of release. This is standard practice for bug-fix releases, not feature launches.
Opportunity 1 (Android): Key Numbers
  • Not a traditional A/B test — before/after measurement with iOS as natural control
  • MDE: 2 ppt improvement. Alpha 0.05, power 0.80
  • Required sample: ~2,400 Android sessions. At 500+ daily sessions: readable in 5-7 days.
  • Leading indicator: auth error rate drops to near-zero within 48 hours of release
Opportunity 2 (FlexPro): Key Numbers
  • True A/B test: 50/50 split at checkout
  • Baseline: 40%. MDE: +2 ppt. Alpha 0.05, power 0.80
  • Required per arm: ~3,800 checkout sessions. Total: ~7,600
  • At 200+ daily checkout sessions: full power in ~5-6 weeks
  • Early call possible at week 3-4 via Bayesian monitoring or sequential testing (mSPRT)
  • Formula: n = (z_a/2 + z_b)^2 × [p1(1-p1) + p2(1-p2)] / (p2-p1)^2
Early Stopping Caveat

Fixed-horizon significance tests with early peeking inflate false positive rates. Use sequential testing or Bayesian monitoring (Statsig, Optimizely) to enable valid early stopping. Do not call the test early based on raw p-values from a two-proportion z-test.
